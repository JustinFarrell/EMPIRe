Research program

Engineering devices, assays, and readout together.

The group works across microfluidics, microfabrication, and biomarker detection, treating chip design, assay flow, and quantitative measurement as one connected research problem.

Microfluidics

Controlled flow at small scales

Designing channel networks, droplet generation schemes, and compact handling architectures for repeatable assays.

Microfabrication

Build strategies that support translation

Developing fabrication and integration methods that move from prototype devices toward robust laboratory platforms.

Biomarker detection

Signal pathways with diagnostic value

Creating low-cost detection workflows that turn biological interactions into measurable outputs for sensing and monitoring.

A representative project linking low-cost hardware adaptation with on-demand droplet generation for microfluidic assays.
